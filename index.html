Origamies:

How to make a teddy-bear

Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.

How to make a chameleon

Chameleleons can move their eyes separately, with each eye having a field of 180 degrees. This means they can see 360 degrees if they need to!

How to make a flying-cicada

Most cicads have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.

How to make a pigeon

Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.

How to make a camel

Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

How to make a panda

Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day eating, mainly bamboo sticks.
